South Africa Economic Indicators Analysis (2015–2024)

Overview

An exploratory data analysis (EDA) of four key South African economic indicators across 40 quarters from 2015 to 2024. The analysis examines how unemployment, inflation, GDP growth, and the repo rate moved over a decade marked by structural economic challenges, a global pandemic, and shifting monetary policy.
Data is sourced from Stats SA and the South African Reserve Bank (SARB).

Business Context

South Africa's economy does not behave by the textbook. Unemployment has remained structurally high regardless of growth cycles, inflation is driven more by supply-side shocks than demand, and monetary policy operates under significant constraints. This analysis explores those dynamics through data.
